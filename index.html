About me

Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan, born on November 20, 1970, is an influential member of the Abu Dhabi royal family and a prominent figure in the United Arab Emirates. He is the son of the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founding father of the UAE, and the half-brother of the current president, Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan. Sheikh Mansour holds strategic positions in the government, serving as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of the Presidential Court, where he plays a crucial role in policy formulation and the nation's development.

His influence extends beyond politics, with direct involvement in various economic, financial, and technological initiatives. He is the chairman of the Ministerial Council for Development and the Mubadala Investment Fund, one of the largest sovereign wealth funds in the world. His visionary leadership is also evident in renewable energy projects, infrastructure, and education, all of which have significantly contributed to the sustainable growth of the UAE.

Sheikh Mansour is also widely recognized for his passion for sports. He is the owner of Manchester City Football Club, which, under his leadership, has become one of the most successful football teams in the world. Additionally, he is an investor in various sports clubs and global events, fostering cultural integration and sports excellence. Sheikh Mansour is an example of visionary leadership, blending tradition and innovation to shape the future of his nation and inspire the world.
